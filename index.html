Code Meets Design

Yara Damerau is a computer science student with a focus on design, and expertise in both frontend and backend development. They has worked on numerous projects at the intersection of computer science and design, blending technical skills with creativity to deliver innovative solutions.

Inclusive platform for culture

The project addressed the exclusion of people with disabilities in the cultural sector, where access to offerings was limited and varied by disability. The goal was to develop a user-friendly platform where accessible cultural events could be easily found. In collaboration with Un-Label e.V., we developed prototypes through participatory work with individuals with disabilities. These results contributed to the creation of a nationwide, barrier-free digital cultural platform.

VinoVirtu

How can we create connectedness over distance? Through communication, but this is often only digital and many shared rituals need another level. VinoVirtu was created from this idea. Custom made wine glasses with which you can toast with your friends even over long distances. The glasses work with Arduinos, sensors and LEDs, which are embedded in the base cut from Plexiglas discs.

IOT
Tape Feel

This piece was created under the theme ‘Hyperlinked Originals - Interface Performances & Conversation Pieces’. Made exclusively from recycled rubbish, it communicates with the viewer on the sound level. The short piece of tape is always looped backwards and creates a monotonous dyssonaz, which gives room for interpretation when listening for a longer time. It was exhibited together with other objects at the Köln International School of Design: KISD.
